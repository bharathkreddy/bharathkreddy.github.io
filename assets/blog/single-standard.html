Hypothesis Testing - Importance!

  • By Bharath k. Reddy
  • March 05, 2020
  • PythonData Analytics

The philosophical and practical debates underlying application of hypothesis testing are,often neglected. The fruitful application of hypothesis testing can benefit from a clear insight into, the underlying concepts and their limitations.

Lets imagine you and your friend play a game. If a coin lands on heads, you win a dollar and if it lands on tails your friend wins a dollar. Now the coin is tossed and lo! 7 times in a row the coin landed on tails !!, now you are thinking - is the coin rigged ? "Could these observations really have occurred by chance?"

Fancy Terminology

    Null Hypothesis: Belief that the observations are the result purely of chance. In our case its a coin being unboiased and 50% probability of heads and 50% probability of tails at eacch toss.
    Alternative Hypothesis: Belief that there is a real effect, that the obervations are the result of this real effect, plus chance variation. In our case this is coin being rigged and the probability is not 50% for heads and tails.
    P-value:A Probability statement which answers the question:if the null hypothesis were true, then what is the possiblity of observing a test statistic at least as extreme as the one we observed? In this case we formulate - what is the probability of observing 7 tails in a row?
    Alpha:a.k.a Significance level. This acts as a cutoff point below which we agree that effect is staistically significant. So if P-value is less than equal to Alpha then we rule out the null hypotheis and agree that something else is going on. In our case lets choose 5% as alpha i.e. if p-value in scenario of fipping tails 7 times in a row is 1 in 128 or 0.8%

Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence.

Carl Segan quoting Gillispie

The rule in determining whether we reject the null hypothsis is simple. If the P-Value is greater than Alpha - Do not Reject. In our case we reject the null hypothesis that coin is not baised.

Are you saying that the coin is rigged? My friend wont do it !!

Here i need to tell you about errors, especially these two types.

    Type 1 error: Rejecting a true null hypothesis. In our case despite all the statistical methods used - if the coin was fair and we rejected the null hypothesis - we would be commiting Type 1 error.
    Type 2 error: Accepting a false null hypothesis. In our case if we agree that the coin was fair but if coin was rigged then we would have committed a type 2 error.

It all boils down to what are your costs of making a type 1 and type 2 errors. Accordingly we set the value of Alpha, which acts as a decision boundary for us to choose when to reject or agree on null hypothesis.It's better that ten guilty persons escape than that one innocent suffer type of thought. Food for thought - if you were deciding on identifying people with Corona virus what woud be yours costs for each type of error ?
To summarize here are the steps in hypothesis testing

  • Formulate Hypothesis.
    • Null hypothesis.
    • Alternative hypothesis.
  • Choose an Alpha based on your costsof Type 1 and Type 2 errors.
  • Find the probability of observation and either accept or reject the null hypothesis.
